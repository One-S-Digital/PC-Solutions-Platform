## Profile Management

Your profile is your resume on the platform. Make it shine!

### Accessing Your Profile

1. Click **Profile** in the sidebar, OR
2. Click your profile picture → **"Edit Profile"**

### Personal Information

| Field | Description | Tips |
|-------|-------------|------|
| **First Name** | Your first name | As it appears on documents |
| **Last Name** | Your last name | Professional name |
| **Email** | Contact email | For employer communication |
| **Phone** | Phone number | Include country code |

### Professional Information

| Field | Description | Tips |
|-------|-------------|------|
| **Short Bio** | Brief introduction | 2-3 sentences about yourself |
| **Work Experience** | Previous positions | List chronologically, most recent first |
| **Education** | Degrees, diplomas | Include institution and year |
| **Certifications** | Professional certifications | Add all relevant certificates |
| **Skills** | Your competencies | Languages, pedagogies, software, etc. |

### Profile Photo & Cover Image

| Element | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| **Profile Photo** | Professional headshot, face clearly visible |
| **Cover Image** | Optional banner image |

### How to Add Certifications

1. Go to Profile → Certifications section
2. Click **"Add Certification"**
3. Enter:
   - Certification name
   - Issuing organization
   - Date obtained
   - Expiration date (if applicable)
4. Save

### How to Add Skills

1. Go to Profile → Skills section
2. Click **"Add Skill"**
3. Type the skill name
4. Select proficiency level (if prompted)
5. Save

**Popular Skills for Educators:**
- German, French, English (languages)
- Montessori, Reggio Emilia (pedagogies)
- First Aid, CPR (safety)
- Early Childhood Development
- Special Needs Support
- Music, Arts, Sports

### Uploading Your CV

1. Go to Profile → CV section
2. Click **"Upload CV"**
3. Select your file (PDF recommended)
4. Save

> 💡 **Tip:** Keep your CV updated and under 5MB.

## Availability Settings

Tell employers when you're available to work.

### Accessing Availability

Go to **Profile** → **Availability** section.

### Employment Type Preferences

Select the types of work you're interested in:

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| ☐ **Full-time** | 100% employment |
| ☐ **Part-time** | Less than 100% |
| ☐ **Temporary (Substitution)** | Short-term replacement |

Check all that apply.

### Weekly Schedule Editor

Set your regular availability:

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Weekly Availability                                 │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                      │
│  Monday    │ ☑ Available │ 07:00 - 18:00           │
│  Tuesday   │ ☑ Available │ 07:00 - 18:00           │
│  Wednesday │ ☑ Available │ 07:00 - 12:00           │
│  Thursday  │ ☑ Available │ 07:00 - 18:00           │
│  Friday    │ ☑ Available │ 07:00 - 18:00           │
│  Saturday  │ ☐ Not Available                        │
│  Sunday    │ ☐ Not Available                        │
│                                                      │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

For each day:
1. Check if you're available
2. Set your available hours

### Date Overrides

Mark specific dates when you're unavailable:

**Adding Unavailable Dates:**
1. Click **"Add Date Override"**
2. Select the date(s)
3. Mark as **"Unavailable"**
4. Add a note (optional): "On vacation"
5. Save

**Adding Special Availability:**
1. Click **"Add Date Override"**
2. Select the date(s)
3. Mark as **"Available"** with custom hours
4. Save

### Availability Notes

Add a general note about your availability:
- "Available to start immediately"
- "Notice period: 1 month"
- "Flexible hours negotiable"

### Preferred Regions

Select the cantons/regions where you're willing to work:
- ☐ Zurich
- ☐ Geneva
- ☐ Basel
- ☐ Bern
- (etc.)

> 💡 **Tip:** The more regions you select, the more job matches you'll see.

### Replacement Availability

Toggle **"Available for replacement shifts"** to enable `availableForReplacement` on your profile. When this is active:

- Foundations posting urgent short-notice cover requests can find and match you
- You can optionally set a **date range** (start/end dates) for when you are available for replacements
- You can set a **radius (km)** to limit offers to shifts within a certain distance

Enabling this setting makes your profile visible in the replacement matching pool. You will receive offers via the platform — see [Replacement Offers](#replacement-offers) for how to respond.

## Applying to Jobs

### How to Apply

1. Find a job you're interested in
2. Click the job to view details
3. Click **"Apply"** or **"Apply Now"**
4. Fill in the application form:
   
   | Field | Required | Tips |
   |-------|----------|------|
   | **Cover Letter** | Optional but recommended | Personalize for each job |
   | **CV** | Optional (or use saved CV) | Keep it up to date |
   
5. Review your application
6. Click **"Submit Application"**

### Writing a Good Cover Letter

A great cover letter should:
- ✅ Be personalized to the specific job
- ✅ Highlight relevant experience
- ✅ Show enthusiasm for the role
- ✅ Be concise (3-4 paragraphs)
- ✅ Include why you're a good fit

**Template:**

```
Dear [Hiring Manager],

I am writing to express my interest in the [Job Title] 
position at [Daycare Name].

With [X years] of experience in early childhood education 
and a [certification/qualification], I am confident in 
my ability to contribute to your team.

I am particularly drawn to your [pedagogy/values/program] 
and believe my skills in [relevant skills] align well 
with your requirements.

I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how I can 
contribute to [Daycare Name].

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

### After Applying

- You'll receive a confirmation
- The application appears in **My Applications**
- Wait for the employer to review
- Check your messages for updates

## My Applications

Track all your job applications in one place.

### Accessing Applications

Click **Applications** in the sidebar.

### Application Statuses

| Status | Meaning | What to do |
|--------|---------|------------|
| ⏳ **Pending** | Submitted, not yet reviewed | Wait for response |
| 👀 **Reviewed** | Employer has seen it | Be patient |
| ⭐ **Shortlisted** | You've been shortlisted — await next steps | Watch for messages |
| 🗓️ **Interview** | Interview scheduled — check messages | Confirm the details |
| 📋 **Offer** | Job offer received — check messages | Review and respond |
| 🎉 **Hired** | You've been hired — congratulations! | Celebrate! |
| ✅ **Accepted** | Moving forward (legacy / general accepted state) | Check messages |

### Application Details

Click an application to see:
- Job details
- Your submitted cover letter
- CV you attached
- Application date
- Current status
- Employer messages

### Withdrawing an Application

Changed your mind?

1. Open the application
2. Click **"Withdraw Application"**
3. Confirm

> ⚠️ **Note:** Once withdrawn, you may not be able to reapply.

## Replacement Offers

When foundations post urgent replacement requests and your profile matches, you may receive replacement shift offers. These appear in the **Replacement Offers** tab of your Applications page.

### Accessing Replacement Offers

1. Click **Applications** in the sidebar
2. Select the **Replacement Offers** tab

### Replacement Match Statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| 🔍 **Proposed** | You've been suggested for a shift (not yet formally offered) |
| 📬 **Offered** | The foundation has sent you a shift offer |
| ✅ **Accepted** | You've accepted the shift |
| ❌ **Declined** | You declined the offer |

### How to Accept or Decline an Offer

1. Open the offer from the **Replacement Offers** tab
2. Review the shift details: role, dates, location, and the foundation
3. Click **Accept** to confirm the shift, or **Decline** to pass
4. Once you accept, the foundation is notified and will confirm the placement
5. Both parties receive a confirmation notification/email when the foundation confirms

### Getting Found for Replacement Shifts

Enable **"Available for replacement shifts"** in **Profile → Availability** (see [Replacement Availability](#replacement-availability)) so foundations can match you to urgent requests.

## Support & Help

### Creating a Support Ticket

1. Click **Support** in the sidebar
2. Click **"Create Ticket"**
3. Fill in:
   - Subject
   - Category
   - Description
4. Submit

### Common Questions

**Q: How long does it take to hear back from employers?**
A: Response times vary. If you haven't heard back in 2 weeks, the position may be filled.

**Q: Can I apply to multiple jobs at the same foundation?**
A: Yes! Apply to any positions you're qualified for.

**Q: How do I know if my application was received?**
A: You'll see it in **My Applications** with a "Pending" status.

## Tips for Success

### Stand Out to Employers

1. **Complete your profile 100%** - Incomplete profiles are often skipped
2. **Upload a professional photo** - First impressions matter
3. **Keep your CV updated** - Add recent experience
4. **Write custom cover letters** - Don't copy-paste
5. **Set accurate availability** - Be honest about your schedule
6. **Respond quickly** - Show you're engaged

### Job Search Tips

1. **Check the job board regularly** - New jobs are posted frequently
2. **Set up filters** - Find relevant positions faster
3. **Apply early** - Popular positions fill quickly
4. **Be flexible** - Consider different contract types
5. **Expand your search** - Consider nearby regions
